History

De Cairo's Mountain & Ocean View Resort in Boljoon, Cebu, was created on June 18, 2018. The name of the resort was inspired by their last name which is Cairo. The owner of De Cairo's Resort is Jun Jun Cairo. It offers a tranquil retreat with stunning mountain and ocean views. Boljoon boasts rich cultural heritage, historic churches, and well-preserved colonial architecture, including the UNESCO World Heritage Site candidate, Boljoon Church.

The owner of De Cairo Mountain Resort is a man shaped by nature and guided by purpose. Raised with simple values and a deep love for the outdoors, he spent his early years exploring mountains, forests, and rivers. His travels across continents strengthened his belief that peace, beauty, and silence are life’s greatest luxuries.

When he discovered the ridge where the resort now stands, he envisioned a sanctuary that blended seamlessly with the landscape. He built it with local materials, hired community artisans, and lived on-site during construction to ensure every detail honored the mountain.

Today, he remains a humble steward walking the trails at dawn, tending gardens, and treating guests and staff like family. His philosophy is clear — “A resort should not just host people it should heal them.” De Cairo Mountain Resort is his legacy — a place where simplicity, nature, and serenity meet.

Success of the Owner

The success story of the owner of De Cairo’s is one rooted in vision, resilience, and an unwavering belief in possibility. Starting with little more than a dream and a determination to create something unique, the owner transformed a modest idea into a thriving brand known for its quality, creativity, and connection to the community.

Challenges of the Founder

Establishing a resort requires creativity in designing attractive amenities, selecting a strategic location, and ensuring that the place reflects comfort, beauty, and a unique experience for guests. It also demands managerial skills—coordinating workers, handling finances, meeting local regulations, and building connections with suppliers and the community.

How will you see the business after 10 years?

After 10 years, the business is expected to be more stable and successful. It will have more loyal customers, improved products and services, and possibly additional branches. The business may use modern technology and online platforms to reach more customers. It will also provide more employment opportunities and contribute to the local economy.

How did the government of the Philippines help your business grow?

The government of the Philippines helped the business grow through financial assistance programs, training and seminars, and support for small and medium enterprises (SMEs). Government agencies provided guidance on proper business registration, taxes, and permits. Programs such as low-interest loans and livelihood training helped the business improve operations and expand.
